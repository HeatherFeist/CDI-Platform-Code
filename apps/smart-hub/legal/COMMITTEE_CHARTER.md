# Business Incubation Committee Charter
## CDI Smart Hub - Turnkey Business Creation Engine

**Effective Date**: [Date]
**Approved By**: Board of Directors

---

## 1. Purpose & Mission

The Business Incubation Committee (the "Committee") is a standing committee of CDI Smart Hub, established to oversee the "Turnkey Business Creation Engine."

**Mission**: To identify, validate, fund, and launch sustainable small businesses that create economic opportunity for our members and revenue for the nonprofit.

---

## 2. Authority & Responsibilities

The Committee is authorized by the Board of Directors to:

### A. Concept Development
1.  Research and propose new business concepts (minimum 4 per cycle).
2.  Develop detailed business plans and financial projections.
3.  Determine the "Funding Goal" (Seed Capital) required for each concept.

### B. Financial Oversight
1.  **Seed Fund Management**: The Committee has the authority to spend funds from a project's "Portfolio Savings Account" *solely* for the items listed in that project's approved Startup Checklist.
2.  **Vendor Selection**: Select and pay vendors for equipment, inventory, and services.
3.  **Audit**: Maintain a transparent ledger of all expenditures for the future owner.

### C. Auction & Transfer
1.  Set the "Minimum Bid" for each auction (typically equal to the Funding Goal).
2.  Approve the final transfer of assets to the winning bidder.
3.  Ensure the new owner signs the "Merchant Coin Liability Agreement."

---

## 3. Membership

The Committee shall consist of **5-7 voting members**, appointed by the Board.

**Required Roles**:
1.  **Chairperson**: Leads meetings and reports to the Board.
2.  **Financial Lead**: Oversees project budgets and spending.
3.  **Legal/Compliance Lead**: Ensures regulatory compliance (LLC formation, etc.).
4.  **Market Research Lead**: Validates business concepts.
5.  **Community Representative**: Represents the interests of donors/bidders.

**Term**: Members serve a 1-year term, renewable.

---

## 4. The "Crowd-Incubation" Process

The Committee shall follow this strict workflow for all projects:

1.  **Ideation**: Committee brainstorms concepts.
2.  **Vetting**: "Go/No-Go" vote on feasibility and risk.
3.  **Board Approval**: Top concepts presented to the Board for final "Green Light."
4.  **Fundraising**: Concept listed on Marketplace for crowdfunding.
5.  **Execution**: Once funded, Committee executes the Startup Checklist (buys assets).
6.  **Auction**: Business is auctioned to members.

---

## 5. Spending Policy (The "Trust" Clause)

To maintain donor trust, the Committee is bound by the following spending rules:

1.  **Ring-Fenced Funds**: Funds donated for "Project A" *cannot* be spent on "Project B" or general nonprofit operations.
2.  **Checklist Adherence**: Funds may only be spent on items explicitly listed in the public "Startup Checklist" unless a variance is approved by the Board.
3.  **Refunds**: If a project fails to reach its Funding Goal within [90] days, donors shall be offered a refund or the option to redirect funds to the General Fund.

---

## 6. Meetings & Quorum

*   **Frequency**: Monthly (or ad-hoc as needed).
*   **Quorum**: A simple majority (51%) of members must be present to vote.
*   **Voting**: Decisions require a majority vote of present members.

---

## 7. Conflict of Interest

Committee members must disclose any financial interest in vendors or potential bidders. Members may *not* bid on businesses they helped incubate without explicit Board approval and recusal from the auction oversight process.
