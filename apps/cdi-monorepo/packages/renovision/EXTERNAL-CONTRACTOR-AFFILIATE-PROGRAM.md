# External Contractor Affiliate Program (Software-Agnostic)

## üéØ Core Concept

**"Got a lead you don't want? Send it to us in 30 seconds, earn 10-15% when we complete it."**

External contractors (like Nick with 20 years experience) don't need to join our platform as members. They stay in their own business, using whatever software they already use (HouseCall Pro, Jobber, ServiceTitan, Excel, etc.), and simply **forward unwanted leads** to earn passive income.

## üß† Why This Model Works

### 1. **No Integration Headaches**
- ‚ùå Don't need API access to HouseCall Pro
- ‚ùå Don't need webhook partnerships with Jobber
- ‚ùå Don't need ServiceTitan enterprise contracts
- ‚úÖ External contractor manually forwards leads = **they ARE the integration**

### 2. **Works With ANY System**
The external contractor could be using:
- Professional software (HouseCall Pro, Jobber, ServiceTitan, Buildertrend)
- Basic tools (Excel, Google Calendar, QuickBooks)
- Old-school methods (Paper notebook, voicemail transcription)
- **Doesn't matter** - if they have a lead they don't want, they can send it to us

### 3. **Project Managers Handle Everything**
- External contractor submits lead ‚Üí done
- **Our project manager** calls the client, qualifies the lead, explains the process
- Client never knows the lead came from another contractor (unless we want to disclose)
- External contractor stays completely hands-off, just collects checks

### 4. **Removes Liability from External Contractor**
Traditional referral scenario:
- Nick refers client to another contractor
- Job goes bad
- Client blames Nick: *"You recommended them!"*

Our model:
- Nick sends lead to Constructive Designs (the organization)
- **We** assign it to a member through competition system
- If job goes bad, client blames us, not Nick
- Nick's reputation stays protected

## üîÑ Lead Flow Process

### Step 1: External Contractor Receives Unwanted Lead
Nick gets a call: *"I need a bathroom remodel in Spokane, $8,000 budget."*

Nick thinks: 
- ‚ùå Too small for my crew
- ‚ùå Wrong city (I'm in Seattle)
- ‚ùå Timeline conflicts with other jobs
- ‚úÖ **Perfect for Constructive Designs members**

### Step 2: Nick Opens Mobile Form (30 Seconds)
He opens the text message link (saved in his phone), fills out:
- **Where:** Spokane, WA
- **When:** ASAP (ideally start next week)
- **Duration:** 2-3 weeks estimated
- **Pay:** $8,000 budget (client's estimate)
- **Notes:** (voice-to-text) "Full bathroom remodel, master bath, wants tile shower, new vanity, toilet, and fixtures. Client name is Sarah, she's ready to go, just needs someone available soon."

**Submits** ‚Üí Done.

### Step 3: Project Manager Liaison Takes Over
Within 1 hour, PM (Project Manager) receives notification:
- **Lead Source:** Nick Thompson (Overflow Partner tier)
- **Lead Details:** [Nick's form data]
- **Status:** Pending Contact

PM calls Sarah (the client):
- "Hi Sarah, this is Jessica from Constructive Designs. I understand you're looking for a bathroom remodel?"
- Qualifies the lead (confirms budget, timeline, scope)
- Explains the competition process: "We have skilled contractors in Spokane who will compete for your project. You'll receive 3-5 proposals within 48 hours."
- Gets client's approval to proceed

### Step 4: Lead Enters Competition System
PM creates the job opportunity in platform:
- **Project Type:** Bathroom Remodel
- **Competition Format:** Sealed Bid (contractors submit proposals, client picks winner)
- **Location:** Spokane, WA
- **Budget:** $8,000
- **Marked as:** External Referral (Nick gets commission if completed)

### Step 5: Members Compete to Win
- 7 Spokane-area contractors see the opportunity
- 4 submit detailed proposals with portfolios
- Client (Sarah) reviews proposals, picks winner (Marcus)

### Step 6: Job Completion & Commission
- Marcus completes the job ($7,800 final cost)
- Client releases payment
- Platform fee: $780 (10%)
- **Nick's commission: $390** (5% for Overflow Partner tier)
- **Platform keeps: $390** (5%)

Nick gets automated payout email: *"Your referral is complete! $390 deposited to your account."*
